Origin story

From an insult to a movement.

On 15 May 2026, Chief Justice of India Surya Kant’s courtroom remarks comparing some unemployed youth and activists to “cockroaches” and “parasites” triggered national outrage. The next day — 16 May 2026 — Abhijeet Dipke launched the Cockroach Janta Party as satire that stuck.

Founder

Abhijeet Dipke

30-year-old political communications strategist and Boston University alumnus (MS Public Relations). Founded CJP on 16 May 2026 in New Delhi.

Status & Scope

Parody & Civil Satire

Not registered with the Election Commission of India (ECI). CJP functions as a youth civic protest platform focusing on systemic unemployment, education leaks, and judicial accountability.

Supreme Court Response

"Don't Take Satire Sentimentally"

When petitions were brought before the Supreme Court seeking investigations into CJP, CJI Surya Kant advised lawyers not to take parody sentimentally and declined urgent intervention.

Origin & CJI Clarification

The Spark & Court Remarks

Sparked by May 15, 2026 court remarks comparing certain activists to "cockroaches & parasites". CJI Surya Kant later clarified the remarks were directed at bogus law degree holders.

On-Ground Action

Jantar Mantar Protests

Evolved from a viral #MainBhiCockroach social media trend to physical youth rallies at Jantar Mantar, New Delhi, demanding student reforms.

Digital Presence

Millions Joined Swarm

Peak Instagram engagement reached 20M+ weekly interactions. Delhi High Court petition challenged IT Act blocking of @cjp_official on X (Twitter).

Trending (July 2026)

Chalo Sansad March & High-Level Talks

On July 20, 2026, CJP launched the "Chalo Sansad" march in Delhi with over 100,000 students protesting NEET exam paper leaks. Following batons/lathicharge and detentions, representatives Saurav Das and Ashutosh Ranka held direct talks with Health Minister J.P. Nadda on July 21 to push the CJP 5-point manifesto.

The Philosophy of Creative Protest

When standard discourse fails, satire steps in. The Cockroach Janta Party is built on the premise that traditional channels of dissent have been clogged by bureaucratic inertia and political capture. By adopting the very name used to denigrate the struggling youth, CJP turns the insult back on the institutions of power. We are not just survivalists; we are the mirror reflecting the systemic failures of education, public sector job allocations, and economic policy.

Our vision is not to govern, but to challenge those who do. Through clever internet memes, viral hashtags, and physical sit-ins, we keep public interest focused on the issues that matter. In a landscape where serious criticism is often suppressed or ignored, satire provides a shield of humor that cannot be easily prosecuted or dismissed.

Beyond the Screen: On-Ground Outreach

What started as a digital-first movement has quickly morphed into a national offline network. CJP coordinators organize local student union chapters, peer-to-peer job counseling, and peaceful demonstrations. Our presence at Jantar Mantar has demonstrated that satirical theater can draw real, passionate crowds who are desperate for changes in exam systems like NEET, UPSC, and state-level public service recruitments.

We believe in direct civil accountability. CJP’s tracker tool keeps the spotlight on pending promises made by ministries and educational boards, ensuring that the media does not move on before the youth receive the answers and reforms they were promised.

Our Long-Term Blueprint for Youth Advocacy

01 / Educational Audit

Demanding full, independent forensic audits of all national examination boards to eliminate paper leaks and corrupt contracting practices.

02 / Unemployment Ticker

Establishing a live-updated public index of real unemployment figures, countering sanitized official metrics with ground-level surveys.

03 / Judicial Accountability

Enforcing strict rules of conduct to ensure that public citizens and students are treated with dignity inside courtrooms, without insults.
