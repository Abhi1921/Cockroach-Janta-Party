  1. 01
    No Rajya Sabha or Post-Retirement Appointments for Retiring CJIs & Judges

    No Chief Justice or senior judge of the Supreme Court or High Courts shall be granted a Rajya Sabha nomination, governorship, or government-appointed post for a mandatory 5-year cooling-off period. This prevents back-channel deals and protects the constitutional independence of the judiciary.

  2. 02
    Voter Roll Integrity & Chief Election Commissioner Personal Accountability

    If any legitimate registered citizen's vote is found deleted from voter rolls on election day, the Chief Election Commissioner and returning officers must face personal legal and criminal prosecution. Administrative errors that disenfranchise voters strike at the heart of democracy.

  3. 03
    Mandatory 50% Representation for Women in Parliament & Central Cabinet

    Women must receive 50% reservation in Lok Sabha, Rajya Sabha, state legislative assemblies, and the central cabinet without expanding parliamentary seat counts. Equal gender representation ensures balanced public policy and empowers the female workforce.

  4. 04
    Anti-Monopoly Media Licensing & Independent Broadcast Protections

    Strict anti-monopoly regulations to prevent corporate conglomerates (like Adani and Reliance) from controlling mainstream news networks. Broadcast airwaves are public assets and must serve independent journalism rather than state or corporate propaganda.

  5. 05
    Strict Anti-Defection Penalties — 20-Year Election Ban for Defecting Representatives

    Any elected MLA or MP who defects to another political party after winning an election must face an automatic, mandatory 20-year ban from contesting local, state, or national elections. This puts an end to resort politics and horse-trading that betray voter mandates.

Judicial Neutrality & Post-Retirement Independence

The judiciary is the ultimate guardian of fundamental rights in India. When senior judges look forward to executive appointments or legislative nominations immediately after retirement, public faith in impartiality is compromised. The Cockroach Janta Party (CJP) demands a statutory 5-year cooling-off period for all retiring judges before accepting any government office.

By eliminating post-retirement political incentives, judges can rule without subconscious bias or fear of administrative retaliation, keeping the doctrine of separation of powers sacred under Article 50 of the Constitution.

#JudicialNeutrality #CoolingOffPeriod

Electoral Integrity & Citizen Disenfranchisement

Elections are the core mechanism of democratic consent. Mass deletions of legitimate voters from voter lists on polling day represent a critical failure of electoral administration. CJP proposes strict statutory liability for Election Commission officials when voter rolls are tampered with or poorly maintained.

Every citizen must have instant digital verification of voter status 30 days prior to elections, with mandatory fast-track courts to restore deleted names before polling begins.

#VoterRights #ElectionAccountability

Corporate Media Ownership & Free Press Integrity

Media consolidation under corporate monopolies distorts public dialogue. When news channels are owned by industrial conglomerates with government contracts, investigative journalism is replaced by state PR and partisan debates.

CJP advocates for independent media trust models, cross-media ownership limits, and public disclosure of government advertising expenditures to ensure democratic reporting remains free from corporate coercion.

#FreePress #MediaIndependence
