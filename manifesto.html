The five-point manifesto.

As summarised in public encyclopaedic reporting, CJP’s formal manifesto includes these demands (satirical political theatre with sharp institutional critique). Additional accepted points include RTI answerability and rejecting a secret “Cockroach CARES” fund.

  1. 01
    No Rajya Sabha for retiring CJIs as reward

    No Chief Justice shall be granted a Rajya Sabha seat as a post-retirement reward. This prevents the erosion of judicial independence and blocks back-channel deals between the judiciary and the ruling party.

  2. 02
    Vote deletion = maximum accountability

    If any legitimate vote is deleted, the Chief Election Commissioner shall face the harshest accountability — framed by CJP as no less than an attack on democratic rights. Voter roll transparency must be absolute.

  3. 03
    50% for women

    Women shall receive 50% reservation (instead of 33%) without increasing Parliament’s strength; 50% of Cabinet positions reserved for women. True equal representation, not tokenism.

  4. 04
    Independent media over conglomerate capture

    Licences of media houses owned by Adani Group and Reliance (“Ambani”) to be cancelled to make space for independent media; investigate “Godi media” anchors’ bank accounts (as stated in manifesto framing).

  5. 05
    Anti-defection with teeth

    Any MLA or MP who defects shall be barred from contesting and holding public office for 20 years. This puts an end to 'resort politics' and horse-trading that undermines electoral mandates.

Deep Dive: Reforming the Judiciary & Elections

The judiciary must remain the ultimate custodian of the constitution, free from the lure of post-retirement sinecures. When judges look forward to legislative nominations or executive postings immediately after retirement, it casts a shadow of bias over their judgments. CJP demands a mandatory cooling-off period of five years for all senior members of the judiciary before they can accept any government-appointed role.

Similarly, our electoral rolls are the foundation of democracy. Random deletions of thousands of citizens from voter lists right before crucial elections cannot be brushed off as clerical errors. If the Election Commission cannot guarantee the safety of every citizen's vote, the leadership must face legal and professional consequences.

Breaking Corporate Control on Public Narrative

A healthy democracy requires a free, competitive press. The consolidation of major news channels, digital platforms, and print media under the ownership of two or three giant conglomerates has stifled diversity of opinion. The Cockroach Janta Party calls for strict anti-monopoly regulations in the media sector.

Furthermore, news anchors who function as public relations agents for the state rather than questioning the administration must be held accountable. CJP believes that public assets—like broadcast airwaves—must serve public welfare, not corporate balance sheets or political propaganda.

On-ground campaigns have also pushed education integrity — including anger over entrance exam paper leaks and calls for ministerial accountability.

🎓 Student Rights & Education Integrity Addendum

Additional core demands articulated during on-ground student protests at Jantar Mantar:

  • NEET & NTA Accountability: Resignation of Union Education Minister over NEET-UG exam paper leaks and systemic NTA testing failures.
  • Exam Crisis Victim Relief: ₹1 Crore financial compensation for families of students who suffered fatal crises due to entrance exam irregularities.
  • Strict Paper Leak Penalties: Non-bailable criminal prosecution for paper leak cartels and blacklisting of compromised testing centers.
