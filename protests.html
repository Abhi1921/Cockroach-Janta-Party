📢 COMPLETED ACTION · 20 JULY 2026

Sansad Chalo March: 100K+ Swarm Hits Capital

On July 20, over 100,000 students and CJP supporters gathered at Jantar Mantar to march to Parliament demanding the resignation of Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan over NEET-UG paper leaks.

Police Clash: Security forces used tear gas, heavy barricades, and lathicharge (batons) to disperse the peaceful crowd. Dozens of student coordinators were detained, leading to minor injuries and internet shutdown in central Delhi.

📍 Location: Jantar Mantar to Parliament, New Delhi ✊ Demand: Audit of NEET 2026 Exams & Resignation of Minister
⚡ ACTION · 21 JULY 2026

CJP Delegation Meets Health Minister J.P. Nadda

CJP representatives Saurav Das and Ashutosh Ranka held a high-stakes meeting with Union Health Minister J.P. Nadda at his residence in New Delhi. The delegation formally presented the 5-point Swarm Manifesto, focusing on student exam safeguards and anti-defection.

Outcome: Minister Nadda assured the CJP that the government would host internal discussions regarding their exam transparency demands and look into releasing detained activist Sonam Wangchuk.

📍 Location: Health Ministry, New Delhi 🔑 Key Reps: Saurav Das, Ashutosh Ranka

Decentralized Student Campus Networks

The strength of the Cockroach Janta Party lies in its decentralized structure. There are no central command structures or political gatekeepers. Instead, college coordinators across Delhi University (DU), Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), Jamia Millia Islamia, and other campuses organize local assemblies autonomously. When a protest is called, information spreads organically through encrypted channels and viral social media posts.

By wearing cockroach masks and carrying humorous, satirical placards, protesters maintain a lighthearted, peaceful atmosphere that defuses tension with law enforcement. This unique methodology has made CJP rallies exceptionally safe and welcoming for first-time student protesters.

A History of Creative Action & Satire

Our past actions include the "Unemployed Cleanup Drive" where students swept the streets around governmental buildings dressed in formal business suits. We also organized the "Voluntary Ticker March" where students walked with live calculators displaying the total number of paper leak victims.

CJP's activism proves that political dissent does not have to be angry to be impactful; it can be smart, creative, and impossible to ignore.

Safety, Discipline & Peaceful Mobilization

Following police action during the 20 July march, CJP reiterated strict non-violent protest protocols. Participants carry national flags, maintain peaceful discipline, and document all events via live video feeds to prevent official misrepresentation.

Our legal defense swarm provides immediate assistance to any detained student volunteer, upholding Article 19(1)(a) and 19(1)(b) constitutional guarantees.
