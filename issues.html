Public Accountability & Open Tracking

Traditional systems thrive on obscurity. When students or young citizens file complaints regarding delayed scholarships, basic campus infrastructure, or unfair administrative fee hikes, they are usually handed a token number and ignored. CJP’s Open Issues Board turns this dynamic on its head. Every issue submitted by our verified members is listed publicly with its real status, creating transparent peer-to-peer accountability.

When an issue is marked as "OPEN," our social media swarm is alerted. We utilize public pressure, targeted campaigns, and right-to-information (RTI) filings to force administration offices to respond. Once an issue is resolved, it is cataloged in our "WON" list as proof that organized community coordination works.

Eradicating Red Tape

Bureaucracy expects people to get tired and move on. CJP’s tracker and issues boards are built on persistent persistence. By cataloging official responses (or lack thereof) and publishing them, we make it impossible for officials to deny knowledge of ground-level problems.

We focus on practical, local challenges: academic stipend clearances, hostel living conditions, campus library hours, and local public transit access for students. Our goal is to empower student bodies to document issues with names, dates, and receipts.
